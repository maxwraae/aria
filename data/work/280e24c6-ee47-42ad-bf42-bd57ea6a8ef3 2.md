# Keep Revolut Standard or Not

## Your situation

You're on **Premium (75 kr/mo = 900 kr/year)**. You use Revolut heavily for spending abroad (US + travel). The question: is it worth 900 kr/year?

## What Premium gives you over Standard

| | Standard (free) | Premium (75 kr/mo) |
|---|---|---|
| Free exchange limit | ~7,400 kr/mo (~€1,000) | **Unlimited** |
| Fee above limit | 0.5% + 1% weekends | None |
| Travel insurance | ❌ | ✅ |
| Purchase protection | ❌ | ✅ up to €1,000 |
| Priority support | ❌ | ✅ |

## The math

Premium pays for itself when you exchange **more than ~22,000 kr/month** abroad:
- 22,000 kr spent → 14,600 kr over Standard's free limit → 0.5% fee = ~73 kr (≈ the monthly cost)
- Weekend spending adds another 1% surcharge on Standard

**Below ~15,000 kr/month abroad:** Standard saves you money. Small occasional fees, but less than 900 kr/year.
**15,000–25,000 kr/month:** Borderline. Insurance might tip it.
**Above 25,000 kr/month:** Premium pays for itself in avoided fees alone.

## Break fee

If you cancel within 10 months: **1 extra month (75 kr)**. That's it — not 2 months. It's tied to the subscription, not card costs (unless you ordered a Metal/Ultra card, which is 330–420 kr).

**75 kr is not worth worrying about.**

## Is the break fee legal in Europe?

**Yes, in its current form.**

- EU Directive 2011/83/EU gives you a **14-day cooling-off period** — cancel any online subscription within 14 days for free
- After 14 days, proportionate fees for services received are allowed
- 75 kr is easily "proportionate" — wouldn't hold up as an unfair term complaint
- Some EU countries (Ireland, France, Sweden) have gotten Revolut to waive break fees; Denmark hasn't
- Revolut is governed by Lithuanian law, but Danish mandatory consumer rules apply to you as a Danish resident
- If it ever felt disproportionate, you could dispute via **Forbrugerklagenævnet**

## Decision

**Figure out your monthly foreign spend through Revolut.** That's the only number that matters.

- Check your Revolut app → Analytics → last 3 months of spending abroad
- If it's consistently under 15,000 kr/month → **drop to Standard**, save 900 kr/year
- If it's over 25,000 kr/month → **keep Premium**, it pays for itself
